### More Info:

Identify any pending Amazon Elasticsearch (ES) Reserved Instances available in your AWS account and solve these incomplete ES reservations by requesting AWS Support to retry the necessary payments

### Risk Level

Low

### Address

Cost Optimisation

### Compliance Standards

CBP

### Remediation

How to remediate if ElasticSearch reserved instances payment is pending?

#### Using AWS Console

1. Contact AWS Support: Reach out to AWS Support for assistance with the pending payment for your ElasticSearch reserved instances. You can contact AWS Support through the AWS Management Console, AWS Support Center, or by phone, depending on your support plan.
   (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "Elasticsearch Reserved Instance Should Not Have Status - Payment Pending" Policy.)
2. Describe the Issue: Clearly describe the problem you are facing, including the specific details about the pending payment. Provide relevant information such as the reserved instance IDs, the billing details, and any other relevant information requested by AWS Support.
3. Provide Account Information: Share your AWS account information with AWS Support, including the account ID and the region where your ElasticSearch instances are located. This will help them locate and identify the specific instances affected by the pending payment.
4. Work with AWS Support: Collaborate with the AWS Support team to resolve the pending payment issue. They will guide you through the necessary steps to remediate the payment, such as updating payment details, initiating the payment, or resolving any underlying billing issues.
5. Monitor Payment Status: After working with AWS Support to address the pending payment, monitor the payment status to ensure that it is successfully processed. Check the AWS Management Console or your account billing information for updates on the payment status.
