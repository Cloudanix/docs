---
title: AWS SES Identities Should Not Be Exposed
---

### More Info:

Your AWS SES identities like domains or email addresses should not be exposed to everyone. This will prevent unauthorized users from sending emails on your behalf and restrict access only to trusted entities by implementing the appropriate AWS SES sending authorization policies.

### Risk Level

High

### Address

Security

### Compliance Standards

CBP, GDPR, APRA, MAS, NIST

### Remediation

How to update the sending authorization policies associated with your Amazon SES identities?

#### Using AWS Console

1. Sign in to the AWS Management Console using an account with appropriate permissions.
2. Open the Amazon SES console by navigating to the SES service.
3. In the left navigation pane, click on "Email Addresses" or "Domains" depending on the type of identity for which you want to update the sending authorization policy.
    (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "AWS SES Identities Should Not Be Exposed" Policy.)
4. Select the specific email address or domain for which you want to update the sending authorization policy.
5. In the identity details page, click on the "Sending Authorization" tab.
6. Under the "Sending Authorization Policies" section, you will see the existing sending authorization policies associated with the identity.
7. To update a sending authorization policy, click on the "Edit" button next to the policy you want to modify.
8. Make the necessary changes to the policy using the policy editor provided by Amazon SES. You can add or remove specific senders or IP addresses, specify sending rate limits, or customize other policy settings.
9. After making the desired changes, click on the "Save" button to save the updated sending authorization policy.
10. Repeat the steps for each additional email address or domain you want to update the sending authorization policy for.
