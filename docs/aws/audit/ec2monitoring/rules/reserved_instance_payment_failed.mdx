---
title: EC2 Reserved Instances Should Not Have Payment Failed
---

### More Info:

To ensure that none of your AWS EC2 Reserved Instance purchases have failed.

### Risk Level

High

### Address

Cost Optimisation

### Compliance Standards

APRA, MAS, AWAF

### Remediation

How to remediate if reserved instance payment has failed

#### Using AWS Console

1. Open the AWS Management Console and navigate to the Amazon EC2 service.
2. From the left navigation pane, click on "Reserved Instances" to view the list of your Reserved Instances.
    (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "EC2 Reserved Instances Should Not Have Payment Failed" Policy.)
3. In the Reserved Instances dashboard, locate the Reserved Instances with a payment failure and make a note of the affected instances.
4. Open the AWS Billing and Cost Management console in a separate browser tab or window.
5. In the AWS Billing and Cost Management console, click on "Bills" from the left navigation pane.
6. Select the appropriate billing period that corresponds to the failed RI payment.
7. Review the billing details to identify the reason for the payment failure. It could be due to an expired payment method, insufficient funds, or other billing issues.
8. Resolve the payment issue based on the cause. For example:
   - If the payment method has expired, update the payment method with valid billing information.
   - If there are insufficient funds, add sufficient funds to the payment method or ensure that the payment method has a valid credit limit.
9. Once the payment issue is resolved, return to the Amazon EC2 service console.
10. Select the affected Reserved Instances by clicking on their checkboxes.
11. From the "Actions" menu above the Reserved Instances list, choose "Modify Reserved Instances".
12. In the "Modify Reserved Instances" dialog box, verify that the payment method is updated and valid.
13. Click on the "Save Changes" button to apply the modified payment details to the Reserved Instances.
