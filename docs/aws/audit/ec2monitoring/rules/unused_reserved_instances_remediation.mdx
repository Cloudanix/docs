### Remediation

How to remediate unused reserved instances?

#### Using AWS Console

1. Identify Unused Reserved Instances:
   - Access the AWS Management Console and navigate to the Amazon EC2 service.
   - From the left navigation pane, click on "Reserved Instances" under the "Instances" section.
   - Review the list of existing Reserved Instances and identify any that are not being fully utilized or are no longer needed.
     (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "Reserved Instances Should Not Be Unused" Policy.)
2. Modify or Sell Unused Reserved Instances:
   - Option 1: Modify:
     - Select the unused Reserved Instance.
     - Click on the "Actions" button and choose "Modify Reserved Instances".
     - Adjust the Reserved Instance attributes, such as instance type, availability zone, or platform, to better match your current needs.
     - Review the modification and click on the "Modify Reserved Instances" button to apply the changes.
   - Option 2: Sell:
     - Select the unused Reserved Instance.
     - Click on the "Actions" button and choose "Sell Reserved Instances".
     - Follow the on-screen instructions to list the Reserved Instance for sale on the Reserved Instance Marketplace.
     - Specify the desired selling price and any additional details requested.
     - Review the listing information and confirm the sale to list the Reserved Instance for sale.
3. Verify Remediation:
   - After modifying or selling the unused Reserved Instances, monitor your environment to ensure that the changes have been applied successfully.
   - Review your billing and usage reports to confirm the impact of the remediation actions on your costs.
