### More Info:

Checks inactivity of any user on a service. Those priviledges should be removed for better security posture.

### Risk Level

Medium

### Address

Security

### Compliance Standards

CISAWS

### Remediation

How to remove privileges to a service if it is unused by an IAM user.

#### Using AWS Console

1. Sign in to the AWS Management Console using an account with administrative privileges.
2. Open the IAM console by navigating to the IAM service.
3. In the navigation pane, click on "Users" to view the list of IAM users in your account.
    (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "User Account Service Inactivity" Policy.)
4. Identify the IAM user(s) for whom you want to remove privileges to a specific service.
5. Select the checkbox next to the IAM user's name to activate the actions menu.
6. Click on the "Permissions" tab in the user details view.
7. Review the policies attached to the user and identify the policy or policies that grant privileges to the unused service.
8. Click on the policy name to edit the policy document.
9. In the policy editor, remove the statements or permissions related to the unused service. You can delete the entire statement or remove specific actions related to the service.
10. Once you have removed the relevant statements or permissions, click on "Review policy" to verify the changes.
11. Review the policy changes and ensure that the desired privileges to the unused service have been removed.
12. Click on "Save changes" to update the policy.
