### More Info:

All the domain names registered with AWS Route 53 or transferred to AWS Route 53 should be renewed 45 days before their validity period ends.

### Risk Level

Low

### Address

Reliability, Security

### Compliance Standards

CBP

### Remediation

How to fix AWS Route 53 Domain which are going to expire in 45 days?

#### Using AWS Console

1. Open the AWS Management Console and navigate to the Route 53 service.
2. In the navigation panel on the left side, click on "Registered domains".
    (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "Route 53 Domain Expiry In 45 Days" Policy.)
3. You will see a list of your registered domains. Locate the domain that is expiring in 45 days and click on its name.
4. On the domain details page, you will find information about the domain, including its expiration date.
5. Click on the "Renew registration" button or the "Renew" link next to the expiration date.
6. In the "Renew registration" window, review the details of the renewal, including the renewal period and the cost.
7. Select the desired renewal period from the dropdown menu. The options typically include 1 year, 2 years, or more.
8. Review the terms and conditions of the renewal and make sure you understand them.
9. Click on the checkbox to acknowledge that you have read and agree to the terms and conditions.
10. Click on the "Renew registration" button to proceed with the renewal.
11. You may be prompted to confirm your payment method and complete the payment process.
12. Once the renewal process is complete, you will receive a confirmation message indicating that your domain has been successfully renewed.
13. It may take some time for the changes to propagate and for the updated expiration date to be reflected in the domain details.
14. After the renewal, make sure to review any other domain settings, such as DNS configurations or contact information, to ensure they are up to date and accurate.
