### Remediation

How to renew Route 53 expired domain (7 Days)?

#### Using AWS Console

1. Log in to the AWS Management Console using your AWS account credentials.
    (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "Route 53 Domain Expiry In 7 Days" Policy.)
2. Navigate to the Route 53 service by selecting "Route 53" from the services menu.
3. In the Route 53 dashboard, click on "Registered domains" in the left navigation pane.
4. Identify the expired domain in the list of registered domains.
5. Take note of the expiration date to determine if the domain can still be recovered.
6. Click on the expired domain to view its details and status.
7. If the domain is still recoverable, you will see an option to "Renew domain registration" or a similar action.
8. Click on the "Renew domain registration" button to initiate the domain renewal process.
9. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the domain renewal process, which may involve confirming or updating your contact information, selecting a renewal period, and making the required payment.
10. After successfully renewing the domain registration, verify that the domain's status is updated to "Active" or a similar indication of a renewed and active domain.
11. If the domain is no longer recoverable or you encounter any issues during the renewal process, you may need to contact AWS Support for further assistance or explore other options such as registering a new domain.
