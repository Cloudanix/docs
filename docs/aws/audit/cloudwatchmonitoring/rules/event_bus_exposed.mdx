---
title: Event Bus Should Not Be Exposed
---

### More Info:

Your AWS CloudWatch event bus should not be exposed to everyone.

### Risk Level

High

### Address

Security

### Compliance Standards

PCIDSS, GDPR, APRA, MAS, NIST

### Remediation

How Event bus should be protected?

#### Using AWS Console

1. Open the AWS Management Console and navigate to the EventBridge service.
2. Click on "Event Buses" in the navigation panel on the left side.
    (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "Event Bus Should Not Be Exposed" Policy.)
3. Identify the event bus that is exposed to the public. Take note of its name.
4. Open the AWS Management Console and navigate to the IAM service.
5. Click on "Policies" in the navigation panel on the left side.
6. Search for and select the policy associated with the public event bus.
7. Click on the "Delete Policy" button.
8. A confirmation dialog will appear. Review the information and click on the "Delete" button to remove the policy.
9. Return to the EventBridge service in the AWS Management Console.
10. Click on "Event Buses" in the navigation panel on the left side.
11. Select the event bus that was exposed to the public.
12. Click on the "Actions" dropdown and select "Edit" to modify the event bus settings.
13. In the "Edit event bus" window, deselect the option to "Allow public access".
14. Click on the "Save" button to apply the changes.
15. Review the other event bus settings and ensure that they align with your desired configuration.
16. Consider implementing additional security measures, such as enabling AWS CloudTrail logging for your EventBridge event bus, to monitor and track any unauthorized access attempts or activities.
17. Regularly monitor your AWS environment for any misconfigurations or unintended exposures to prevent similar issues in the future.
