title: RDS Reserved Instances Should Not Have Status - Payment Failed
---

### More Info:

Identify any failed RDS Reserved Instances (RIs) available within your AWS account. A failed RDS RI is an unsuccessful reservation that received the "payment-failed" status during the purchase process.

### Risk Level

High

### Address

Cost Optimisation

### Compliance Standards

CBP

### Remediation

How to identify any failed RDS Reserved Instances (RIs) within your AWS account?

#### Using AWS Console

1. Log in to the AWS Management Console using your AWS account credentials.
    (In the Cloudanix Console, navigate to "Misconfig" page and look for Affected Assets for "RDS Reserved Instances Should Not Have Status - Payment Failed" Policy.)
2. Navigate to the Amazon RDS service by selecting "RDS" from the services menu.
3. In the RDS dashboard, click on "Reserved Instances" in the left navigation pane.
4. In the "Reserved Instances" page, you will see a list of your active reserved instances.
5. Identify the reserved instance(s) for which the payment has failed.
6. Take note of the specific reservation ID(s) associated with the failed payment.
7. Open a new browser tab and go to the AWS Support Center at https://console.aws.amazon.com/support/home.
8. In the AWS Support Center, click on the "Create case" button.
9. In the "Create case" page, select the "Service Limit Increase" category.
10. In the "Limit Type" dropdown menu, choose "Reserved Instances".
11. Provide a description of the issue, mentioning that the payment has failed for the specified reservation ID(s) and you would like assistance in resolving the payment issue.
12. Click on the "Submit" button to create the support case.
13. AWS Support will review the case and work with you to resolve the payment issue.
14. Monitor your support case for updates and follow any instructions provided by AWS Support.
15. Once the payment issue is resolved, AWS Support will inform you, and the reserved instance(s) will be reinstated.
